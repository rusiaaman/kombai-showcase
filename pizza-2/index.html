The Experience

Three Days of
Pure Artistry

From dawn flour selection to the final char of a perfect cornicione — every moment is a lesson in mastery.

Day One · The Dough
01

The Living Dough

Discover the alchemy of flour, water, salt, and time. You will learn to read fermentation by touch, build gluten structure by hand, and understand why Neapolitan dough breathes differently from any other in the world.

  • Selecting 00 flour and understanding protein content
  • Traditional hand-kneading & windowpane test
  • 72-hour cold fermentation protocol
  • Ball formation & resting science
02

The Sacred Fire

Fire management is not a skill — it is a language. You will learn to build, maintain, and read the wood-fired oven at 485°C, understanding how radiant heat, floor temperature and flame arc create the perfect leopard-spotted crust.

  • Wood selection & fire architecture
  • Floor temperature mapping & management
  • The 60–90 second window: reading the bake
  • Recovery techniques between pizzas
Day Two · The Oven
Day Three · The Soul
03

The Neapolitan Soul

The simplest pizza is the hardest to perfect. On day three, you source ingredients from the Mercato di Porta Nolana at dawn, visit a local mozzarella producer in the countryside, and assemble your masterwork under the open Neapolitan sky.

  • San Marzano DOP tomatoes — selection & preparation
  • Fior di latte vs. buffalo mozzarella masterclass
  • Topping philosophy: less is more
  • Final tasting & critique dinner

Hand Technique

The Neapolitan slap-stretch method. No rolling pins. No shortcuts. Pure feel and muscle memory.

Your Maestro

Born in the Quartieri.
Forged by Fire.

Antonio De Luca learned to make pizza before he learned to read. The grandson of a Quartieri Spagnoli pizzaiolo, he spent his formative years at the ancestral forno in Via Toledo before training under three Michelin-starred chefs across Italy and France.

In 2013, he founded this masterclass as a declaration: that the world's most beloved food deserved the same reverence as haute cuisine. Since then, 144 students from 41 countries have passed through his oven — each leaving with changed hands and a changed perspective.

Antonio holds the highest certification from the Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana and serves as a judge at the annual World Pizza Championship in Parma.
