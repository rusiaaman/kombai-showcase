The Treaty

A Sacred Pact
Between Teacher & Student

Once a year, in the shadow of Vesuvius, twelve students are chosen to enter the most intimate and demanding pizza laboratory in the world. There are no shortcuts. No machinery. No compromise. Only fire, flour, water, salt — and the accumulated wisdom of four generations of Neapolitan masters.

Maestro Enzo Ferrante · Napoli, 1974
The Teacher

Maestro
Enzo Ferrante

Fourth-Generation Pizzaiolo · Napoli

Born in the Quartieri Spagnoli of Naples, Enzo Ferrante learned the craft at age seven beside his grandfather's wood-fired oven. His technique — a 72-hour cold fermentation, Caputo 00 flour, and a single sustained rotation — has been recognised by the Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana as one of the last truly unbroken lineages of traditional pizza-making. He teaches once a year. He accepts twelve students.

This is not a class. It is a treaty. A commitment to preserve what the world is slowly forgetting.

The Curriculum

Six Disciplines.
One Philosophy.

Each module is a chapter in the ancient text of Neapolitan tradition.

01

Il Lievito

The Living Dough

A deep study of wild fermentation — reading dough activity by sight, touch and smell. Understanding hydration ratios, temperature sensitivity and the sacred 72-hour cold proof that defines Neapolitan structure.

02

La Stesura

The Opening

Hand-stretching is a language. This module teaches the precise pressure, spiral motion and wrist technique that preserves the cornicione air pockets — the hallmark of an authentic Neapolitan base.

03

Il Pomodoro

The Sauce

San Marzano DOP tomatoes, hand-crushed. No cooking, no added sugar — only sea salt and the tomato's own acid. Students learn to identify ripeness by aroma, weight and skin tension before a single tomato is opened.

04

La Mozzarella

The Milk

Fior di latte vs. mozzarella di bufala — the choice, the placement, the moisture management. Students practice the pull and the tear; the uneven distribution that creates the perfect melt.

05

L'Olio

The Pour

The final drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil is not decoration — it is the finishing signature. Students learn the spiral pour, quantity judgement by eye, and the philosophy of restraint: one movement, no correction.

06

Il Forno

The Fire

Reading a wood-fired oven requires years. This module compresses the essentials: fire position, dome temperature assessment (without a thermometer), launching technique, rotation timing, and knowing — by sound and colour — when to retrieve.
